Abstract

Representing and reconstructing scenes from monocular videos is a challenging problem in computer vision. Some methods have been proposed to mitigate this issue. However, most previous work has only considered either the dynamic part or the static part, which leads to artifacts without inaccurate motion constraints. In this work, we propose DynamicGaussian that effectively handles video scenes by decomposing the video into static and dynamic components and modeling them independently using 4D Gaussian splatting techniques. Specially, the core of our method lies in a novel 4D Gaussian representation that applies 4D Eulerian angles, providing a robust foundation for encoding scene geometry and appearance with fewer parameters and achieving efficient results. In addition, to enhance the ability to capture the trajectories of dynamic objects, a novel optical flow-based approach is introduced to model dynamic components by leveraging a 2D prior to constrain 3D motion. The depth information is also integrated to refine the fusion of static and dynamic parts. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our reconstructed video has PSNR and LPIPS improvements over existing sota methods on Dynamic Scene dataset and Nerfies dataset.
